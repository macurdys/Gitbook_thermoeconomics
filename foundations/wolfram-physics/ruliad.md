# The Ruliad

> **Primary source:** Wolfram, "The Concept of the Ruliad" (2021) — [writings.stephenwolfram.com](https://writings.stephenwolfram.com/2021/11/the-concept-of-the-ruliad/)

## The Concept

The **Ruliad** is the entangled limit of all possible computations. It is the object you get when you apply all possible rules to all possible initial conditions and trace all possible paths of execution simultaneously.

It is not a model of the universe — it is the ultimate abstract structure from which all possible models, all possible observers, and all possible physical laws can be derived as projections or "slices."

## Why It Matters

The Ruliad provides a framework for understanding why computation, physics, and mathematics are not separate domains but aspects of the same underlying structure:

- **Physics** is what you see when you are an observer embedded in the Ruliad
- **Mathematics** is what you can prove about the Ruliad from the outside
- **Computation** is the process by which the Ruliad generates itself

In this framework, the question "why does proof of work work?" becomes: proof of work is a physical process that exploits the computational irreducibility of the Ruliad — it harnesses the fact that certain computations must be *done* and cannot be *shortcut*.

## Connection to Thermoeconomics

If the Ruliad is the ultimate computational structure, then thermodynamic dissipation (energy use, entropy production) is the cost of navigating it. Proof of work is a navigation protocol: miners explore the Ruliad (the space of all possible hashes) and report back coordinates (valid block hashes) that satisfy specified constraints (difficulty target).

The economic value of the resulting tokens is a measure of the computational work required to find those coordinates — which is itself a measure of the irreducible structure of the Ruliad in the neighborhood of the chosen hash function.
